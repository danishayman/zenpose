ABSTRACT 
The Fast -Fit mobile application aims to support users who is beginner  in adopting 
intermittent fasting (IF) by providing personalized fasting recommendations and users-
friendly tracking tools. As an enhanced version of a previous project, this app 
incorporates a new AI -driven recommendation system that suggests optimal IF plans 
tailored to individual characteristics, such as age, weight, height, and lifestyle. 
Developed in Android Studio with Java and Firebase as the backend database, Fast-Fit

set notifications, and monitor their fasting status in real -time to help them maintain 
consistency. The machine learning component, developed in Python, uses relevant 
datasets to ensure accurate recommendations, making it easier for users to find a fasting 
plan that suits their needs. Fast-Fit ultimately seeks to simplify the process of following 
an IF plan, promoting healthier eating habits and empowering users to achieve their 
wellness goals more effectively.

1 INTRODUCTION 
1.1. Background 
Intermittent fasting (IF) has emerged as a highly popular dietary approach for managing 
weight, enhancing health, and improving lifestyle. Several methods of IF exist, 
including daily time-restricted eating, periodic fasting, and alternate -day fasting, each 
offering unique benefits. However, despite its widespread use, many individuals 
struggle to select the IF method that best aligns with their specific health goals, lifestyle, 
and personal preferences. This difficulty often leads to inconsistent results, reduced 
motivation, or even health complications such as nutrient deficiencies or fatigue. 
Current solutions in the market primarily offer static IF schedules, typically based on 
generic guidelines, which fail to adapt to the unique needs and goals of users. Many 
existing IF apps allow users to track their fasting duration but they do not incorporate 
personalized recommendations based on key users information such as age, weight, 
height, activity level, eating habits and overall health status. This lack of customization 
has been identified as a limitation in helping users choose the optimal fasting plan for 
their individual circumstances. 
Recent advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) 
techniques have revolutionized the way personalized recommendations can be 
delivered in health and wellness apps. AI models such as decision trees, random forests, 
support vector machines and neural networks, have been widely adopted in applications 
ranging from personalized fitness plans to nutritional advice. These AI models leverage 
large datasets containing user profiles and behavioral data to provide dynamic 
recommendations tailored to an individual’s unique characteristics. 
In this context, the Fast -Fit application intends to use machine learning to address the 
gap in the market by offering personalized IF plan recommendations  for the beginner 
level as some of them might having difficulty in selecting the right IF method as they 
need to consider few factors like their lifestyle, schedule and comfort level with fasting 
duration. By collecting key user’s inputs such as age, weight, height, physical activity 
level, and dietary habits, Fast-Fit aims to train a predictive model capable of suggesting

1.2. Problem Statements 
Despite the growing popularity of intermittent fasting (IF) for weight management and 
health improvement, many individuals struggle to select the most suitable fasting 
method for their unique profiles  especially the beginner. Selecting the incorrect IF 
technique can discourage users from reaching their health objectives by producing 
inconsistent outcomes, negative health repercussions, and frustration. This challenge is 
compounded by a lack of personalized guidance in existing IF applications, which 
typically offer only static tracking of fasting cycles without tailoring recommendations 
to individual characteristics such as age, weight, height, activity level, or lifestyle.  
Users are left to explore with numerous fasting strategies without direction, risking 
inefficient or dangerous fasting cycles in the absence of individualized insights. This 
disparity emphasizes the necessity for an intelligent approach that uses artificial 
intelligence (AI) to forecast and recommend appropriate IF approaches based on each 
user’s individual profile in addition to tracking fasting. By utilizing AI, Fast -Fit will 
enable users to quickly determine the best fasting regimens suited to their unique needs, 
enhancing their fasting experience and encouraging healthier results.

1.3. Motivation 
The motivation for this project is from an intent to assist beginners who desire to 
implement intermittent fasting (IF) into their lifestyle.  Many people who are new to 
intermittent fasting have difficulty selecting the best fasting plan since they must 
evaluate factors such as their lifestyle, daily routine, and degree of comfort with fasting 
length.  These considerations can make it challenging to select a sustainable and 
effective approach. 
Everyone is different when it comes to health and fitness.  Each person has a unique 
physical and metabolic profile, which implies that diets and exercise work differently 
for them. For example, not all thin people are free from cholesterol problems, and n ot 
all overweight people are at risk. This shows that we cannot judge someone’s health 
just by their appearance. Therefore, it is important to have a customized approach that 
considers each person's unique characteristics and health goals. 
This project, Fast-Fit, is designed for beginners who desire to incorporate intermittent 
fasting into their daily life.  The purpose is to offer a set of common IF plans for 
beginners while also recommending appropriate plans depending on user’s details like 
as age, weight, and lifestyle.  The app uses artificial intelligence to forecast which 
fasting plan is most suited to the user’s input.  This reduces confusion when choosing a 
plan and increases the chances of success for those who are just starting the ir fasting 
journey. 
1.4. System Objectives  
The main objectives of the Fast-Fit app are as follow:  
1. To provide a popular of time restricted IF plans option for beginner and display 
the real time fasting status. We focus on 3 fasting methods which are 12:12, 
14:10 and 16:8. It allowing users to select options that best match their 
preferences and capabilities, while promoting effective and sustainable fasting 
practices.

1.5. Proposed Solutions 
By combining tracking capabilities with intelligent suggestions catered to each user's 
particular traits, the Fast -Fit app seeks to offer a personalized intermittent fasting 
experience. The solution comprises several key components and technologies:   
1. Mobile Application Development: Android Studio and Java will be the main 
programming languages used in the development of Fast -Fit as an Android 
application. Java is the best option for creating a dependable and responsive 
mobile application because of its effectiveness and compatibility with Android 
smartphones.  
2. Database Integration: Firebase will be used as the backend database to handle 
and store important users’ data, such as users’ profiles. Firebase provides safe 
storage and real -time data synchronization, guaranteeing consistency and 
enabling smooth device updates.  
3. Intelligent Fasting Plan Recommendation: The core feature of Fast -Fit is its 
ability to suggest suitable intermittent fasting plans based on individual user’s 
data. This will be achieved by incorporating a Machine Learning model  which 
is Decision Tree (DT) model to train and predict the most appropriate fasting 
plan. It will be implemented as follows:  
 
• Model Overview: 
The Decision Tree model is a machine learning algorithm that classifies 
users into the appropriate IF plans by analyzing key users attributes such 
as age, weight, height,  and lifestyle. It works by creating a tree -like 
structure of decision rules, where each branch represents a possible

decision based on the users’ data, leading to a final recommendation  
which is the suggestion of the IF plan. 
• Training the Model: 
The DT model will be developed in Python using libraries such as scikit-
learn. A dataset containing anonymized users’ profiles and their 
corresponding IF plans will be used for training. The dataset will 
undergo preprocessing to handle missing values and normalize input 
data. The training process will focus on optimizing the model’s accuracy 
through hyperparameter tuning , such as adjusting the maximum depth 
and minimum samples per leaf. 
• Integration into the App: 
After training, the Decision Tree model is manually implemented in the 
Android app using Java. Instead of using machine learning libraries like 
TensorFlow Lite, the decision rules from the trained model are extracted 
and translated into conditional stateme nts (e.g. if-else logic) within the 
app. This allows the model to process users input such as age, weight, 
and lifestyle directly on the device, enabling fast and efficient fasting 
plan recommendations without relying on external servers or additional 
libraries. 
• Users Interaction: 
Users will input their profile details through the app interface. The 
decision rule will analyze the input data and classify the users into a 
suitable IF plan and display the result on the app.

2.5. Brief Introduction of the Proposed Work 
The proposed work is the Fast-Fit application, an Android-based mobile app designed 
to assist IF beginner in selecting the suitable Intermittent Fasting plan based on their 
individual characteristics such as age, weight, height, and lifestyle. In addition to 
helping user choose the best fasting plan, Fast-Fit also tracks the user's fasting schedule 
and provides timely alerts to ensure they adhere to their chosen fasting routine. The app 
offers beginner popular time-restricted eating of the fasting plans such as 12:12, 14:10 
and 16:8, allowing users to select the plan that best matches their goals and preferences. 
To achieve this, Fast-Fit will incorporate with Machine learning (ML) Classification 
algorithm to perform prediction. In this project we will use Decision Tree (DT), which 
will be trained and tested using a relevant dataset to generate fasting plan 
recommendation. This model evaluates the user's input data and recommends the 
appropriate fasting schedules, promoting effective and sustainable fasting practices for 
beginner. Through this AI-powered recommendation system, Fast-Fit aims to enhance 
the users experience by providing personalized recommendations and real -time 
support, making intermittent fasting easier and more accessible for everyone. 
2.5.1. Intermittent Fasting Method for Beginner 
There are several IF approaches including time-restricted fasting, alternate-day fasting, 
the 5:2 method, and the 24-hour weekly fast.  However, these methods might be difficult 
for beginners because they sometimes demand people to go without eating for long 
periods of time.  For people new to IF, the rigid schedules may be difficult to follow, 
resulting in frustration or discontinuation. Therefore, time-restricted fasting is a better 
option for beginners since it is easier to follow and less challenging  to everyday 
routines.  The 12:12 plan is the most users-friendly plan for beginners, in which the 
individual fasts for 12 hours and eats for the remaining 12 hours of each 24-hour cycle.  
This is because, much of the fasting happens while sleeping, making it easier to handle.  
For example, a person may choose eating at 8:00 p.m. and resume eating at 8:00 a.m. 
the following day.  This method allows the users to maintain their normal daily calorie 
intake while gradually adapting to the fasting pattern. Once the y are comfortable with 
the 12-hour fast, they can slowly extend the fasting period to 14 hours (14:10) or 16 

===== PAGE 27 =====

hours (16:8). These gradual changes support a smoother and more sustainable transition 
into longer fasting windows. 
In the context of the Fast -Fit app, understanding the suitability of each IF plan is 
essential.  A one-size-fits-all strategy is not optimal because each user's lifestyle, health 
objectives, and metabolic demands are unique.  By implementing a personalized 
suggestion system that predicts the appropriate fasting schedule based on users’ 
characteristics, the app allows beginners to begin with a plan that matches their ability 
and subsequently develop at their own pace.  This not only increases users’ satisfaction, 
but it also improves intermittent fasting adherence and long-term success rates. 
2.5.2. Machine Learning Classification Algorithms 
Machine learning classification algorithms fall under supervised learning techniques 
where a model learns from labeled training data to make predictions on new data. These 
algorithms are commonly used to predict categorical outcomes by analyzing patterns in 
input features. In the context of health and fitness applications, classification models 
can play an important role in offering personalized guidance by categorizing users 
based on their attributes. 
One of the most widely used classification algorithms is the Decision Tree. A Decision 
Tree is a model that makes decisions in the form of a tree structure  that consist of root 
node, branches, internal node and leaf node. E ach internal node represents a test on a 
specific feature (e.g., age or weight), each branch represents the outcome of the test, 
and each leaf node provides the final prediction or classification result. Decision Trees 
are highly popular due to their simplicity and interpretability. They can be used for both 
classification and regression tasks where it handles both numerical and categorical data 
and require minimal data preprocessing. Another advantage is that they are easy to 
debug, as each decision path can be clearly traced and understood, making it easier to 
identify errors and improve model performance. 
In this project, the Fast -Fit app utilizes the Decision Tree algorithm to recommend a 
suitable IF plan for users based on their personal characteristics. These include features 
such as gender, age, weight, height, physical activity level, sleep duration, work type,  
work hour  and fitness goals. The model was trained using a dataset labeled with

that run locally on the device. 
By integrating the Decision Tree model in this way, the app is able to provide fast, 
personalized fasting plan recommendations directly on the user’s device without relying

3.5.2. Questionnaire 
To train the machine learning model for the Fast-Fit app, an online survey will be 
conducted to gather data from participants who have experience with IF. The primary 
goal of this questionnaire will be to collect relevant information about users' fasting 
habits, preferences, and post -IF experiences. This data will be used to train the AI 
model, enabling it to provide personalized intermittent fasting plan recommendations 
to users. 
The survey will target adults aged 20 to 60 years who have practiced intermittent 
fasting, ensuring that the data collected is pertinent to the app’s focus on providing 
recommendations based on individual fasting routines. A total of 19 questions will be 
designed is divided into three key sections as shown in Figure A: 
I. Section 1: Information Before IF (Questions 1–12) 
This section will gather essential demographic and lifestyle information from the 
participants before they begin intermittent fasting. It will ask questions about their age, 
weight, height, daily physical activity, sleep time, type of work, health problems, and

participant's health and habits prior to starting IF. 
II. Section 2: IF Plan Being Followed (Questions 13–14) 
 In this section, participants will be asked about the specific intermittent fasting plan 
they follow and their main goals for practicing IF. This information will help categorize 
the different fasting plans and align them with the participants' objectives , such as

3.7.2. Description of Intelligent Method 
The Fast-Fit application will integrate a machine learning (ML) model to provide 
personalized IF plan recommendations to users. The intelligent method used in this 
solution involves supervised learning, ideal for predicting outcomes based on input 
data. The core algorithm will be a Decision Tree (DT) , which will process users’ 
attributes such as weight, height, lifestyle activity, and work style to determine the most 
suitable IF plan. The model will be trained using a dataset gathered from a 
questionnaire, and it will predict the best fasting plan by prioritizing and filtering the 
attributes based on their influence on the target variable, which is the IF plan 
recommendation. 
The DT algorithm will filter and rank the attributes from highest to lowest priority 
towards the target column (IF plan). By evaluating the relationships between the input 
features and the target, the model will make data -driven predictions. The decision tree

4.2. Implementation of Intelligent Method  
4.2.1. IF Plan Recommendation  
The intelligent component of the Fast -Fit application is the IF  plan recommendation 
system which is implemented using a DT classification model. This model was 
developed using the Python programming language within Google Colaboratory, an 
online platform that supports Jupyter Notebook. Google Colab oratory is particularly 
useful as it allows code to be executed line by line that making it easier to identify and 
debug any errors during model development. 
The DT model is designed to classify users into one of three suitable intermittent fasting 
plans, 12:12, 14:10, or 16:8. These plans represent different fasting and eating window 
durations. The classification process is based on various user attributes such as age, 
weight, height, physical activity level, and lifestyle. These features are used as inpu ts, 
while the IF plan labels serve as the class outputs in the training dataset.  Before the 
model can make accurate predictions, it must be trained on a labele d dataset. The 
training process involves train the model with user data and corresponding IF plan 
categories that will allow it to learn decision rules that map input features to the correct 
fasting plan. Once trained, the model is evaluated using a test dataset to assess its 
accuracy and effectiveness in recommending appropriate IF plans. By analyzing 
personal attributes and recommending a suitable fasting schedule, the system provides 
personalized guidance that will make easier for users to start and stick with a plan that 
matches their lifestyle and health goals.

2) Data Preprocessing 
After preparing the dataset, the next critical step is data preprocessing, which

‘Type_of_Work’, ‘Daily_Work_Hours’, ‘Main_Goal’, ‘Height_group’, and 
‘Weight_group’ as the attribute columns while ‘IF_Plan’ as the target column 
for prediction. To ensure the dataset could be interpreted by the DT algorithm, 
categorical data were converted into numerical form through encodi ng. Label 
encoding was applied to each column to map textual data to numeric values,

3) Data Splitting 
The next step is splitting the dataset into training and testing datasets which is 
essential for evaluating the model's ability to generalize to unseen data. In this 
process, the dataset was divided using an 80:20 ratio where 80% of the data 
(1,598 rows) was allocated for training and the remaining 20% (400 rows) was 
set aside for testing. The training set is used to train the DT model to identify 
patterns and relationships between input attributes and the target label while the 
testing set is used to assess the model’s performance on new, unseen instances. 
It is important to ensure that the test data remains hidden during the training 
phase to simulate real world scenarios where the model must make predictions 
on data it has not encountered before. This split helps validate that the model is 
not simply memorizing the training data but is truly capable of making accurate 
recommendations based on learned patterns. 
 
4) Model Training 
The model training stage involved fitting the dataset into DT classifier using the 
entropy criterion. The entropy method is chosen over the Gini index because it 
provides a more detailed measure of information gain which can lead to better 
decision making when the dataset contains multiple classes or when the 
distribution of data is imbalanced. Entropy quantifies the impurity or 
unpredictability in the dataset and aims to split the data in a way that reduces

this uncertainty as much as possible. This can result in a more accurate model, 
especially when used in combination with techniques like class balancing and 
careful parameter tuning. 
The training process is implemented using a train_using_entropy3 method that 
utilizes the ‘DecisionTreeClassifier’ from the ‘sklearn’ library with entropy as 
the splitting criterion. Key parameters were configured to optimize model 
performance and avoid overfitting . The ‘max_depth’ was set to 7 to limit the 
complexity of the tree, ‘min_samples_leaf’ was set to 9 to ensure each leaf node 
has a sufficient number of samples, and ‘min_samples_split’ was increased to 
15 to prevent overly specific splits. Additionally, ‘class_weight='balanced'’ was 
used to compensate for any class imbalances in the dataset, and a fixed 
‘random_state’ ensured reproducibility. Figure 11 illustrates a snippet of the 
training cod e that setup  the entropy-based decision tree. This configuration 
helps the model to generalize better and make more reliable intermittent fasting 
plan recommendations.

IF plan that allow users to efficiently monitor their current fasting or eating status

of their fasting schedule along with progress updates. These notifications are stored 
locally and displayed in the Notification List Page (Figure C.5).

processed using a manually implemented Decision Tree algorithm written in Java. 
Based on the input, the app recommends the suitable IF plan which is displayed on the 
screen (Figure C.12) where it helps users to determine the plan that best matches their 
attributes.

input their weight which is stored and visualized as a line graph (Figure C.15). By 
default, the app reminds users every seven days to update their weight ( Figure C.17).

then displayed using text and an indicator (Figure C.18).

requirements. The Fast -Fit app successfully integrates user inputs, IF plan 
recommendations, progress tracking, and a user -friendly interface. All core functions

5.5. Critical Evaluation 
The Fast-Fit application introduces several innovative elements that distinguish it from 
existing intermittent fasting apps such as Easy Fast. One of its key advantages is the 
integration of an AI -powered recommendation system which uses a Decision Tree 
algorithm to suggest suitable IF plans based on user attributes such as age, weight, 
height, physical activity level and work type. This personalized guidance addresses a 
significant limitation in Easy Fast which does not utilize machine learning to 
recommend fasting plans. As a result, Fast -Fit offers a more tailored and supportive 
experience especially for beginners who may be unfamiliar with which fasting method 
suits their lifestyle and health goals. 
Another strength of Fast-Fit is its offline functionality in the recommendation module. 
The decision logic from the trained model is manually implemented using conditional 
statements in the Android app where it avoids from continuous internet access when 
generating IF suggestions. This design ensures faster response times and improved 
accessibility for users in areas with limited connectivity. 
However, while Fast -Fit offers some advantages, it also has certain limitations when 
compared to Easy Fast. For instance, although Fast -Fit includes visualizations such as 
weight trends and fasting statistics, these features are also present in Easy Fast w hich 
provides similar dashboard capabilities with engaging graphs and health metrics. 
Therefore, in terms of data visualization and progress tracking, both applications offer 
comparable functionality and Fast-Fit does not present a clear advantage in this area. 
Additionally, the scope of the Fast -Fit app remains relatively narrow. It focuses 
primarily on beginner IF plans, which is specifically for 12:12, 14:10, and 16:8 IF plan, 
whereas Easy Fast supports a wider variety of fasting styles including  other time -
restricted IF plan,  OMAD and alternate -day fasting. This limited range may restrict 
Fast-Fit's usefulness for users who wish to explore or transition into more advanced 
fasting methods. 
Moreover, Fast -Fit depends heavily on accurate user input to generate meaningful 
recommendations. Incorrect or incomplete data can result in less effective fasting plan 
suggestions. Unlike Easy Fast, which includes supplementary features such as water

recommendations. Nevertheless, the app's effectiveness could be further enhanced by 
expanding the variety of fasting plans  by introducing more comprehensive health 
tracking tools and refining the user interface to match or surpass the polish of more 
mature applications like Easy Fast.

7 CONCLUSION & FUTURE WORK 
7.1. Conclusion 
The Fast -Fit mobile application was developed with the primary goal of assisting 
beginners in adopting and maintaining IF routines through personalized 
recommendations and effective tracking tools. The application successfully meets the 
key functional requirements outlined during the planning phase. These include offering 
a selection of beginner -friendly IF plans (12:12, 14:10, 16:8), integrating a Decision 
Tree model for IF plan recommendations, tracking user progress through fasting 
statistics and weight trends, calculating BMI, and providing timely reminders through 
notifications. 
All major modules were implemented and tested to be functioning as intended. The use 
of Firebase as the backend ensured secure data storage and smooth data retrieval while 
Java and Android Studio provided a solid foundation for building a responsive and 
reliable mobile application. The integration of the DT model directly into the 
application using rule-based logic allowed offline accessibility for the recommendation 
feature. 
Compared to the initial objectives, the project can be considered successful as all of it 
has been achieved. The application is fit for its intended purpose and demonstrates real 
potential for use in health and lifestyle management. 
 
7.2. Future Work 
While the Fast-Fit application successfully meets its core objectives there are several 
areas for further enhancement that could significantly improve its functionality, 
flexibility, and overall user experience. 
One of the most important areas for future development is the expansion of fasting plan 
options. Currently, the application focuses on beginner -friendly plans such as 12:12, 
14:10 and 16:8. To support users as they progress in their intermittent fasting jo urney,

Additionally, integrating Fast-Fit with external health APIs and wearable devices such 
as Google Fit or smartwatches could greatly enhance data accuracy and reduce manual 
input. Through this integration, real -time tracking of physical activity, sleep durat ion, 
heart rate and calories burned could be used to provide more informed and dynamic 
fasting plan recommendations tailored to each user's current health status and daily
